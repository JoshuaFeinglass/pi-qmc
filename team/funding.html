Sponsors and Funding Support

    Research Grants

    Method development, testing, and applications of the pi-qmc research code has been supported in part by the following grants:

    NSF (2003-present)

    NSF OCI 1148502 Shumway (PI) 06/01/12 – 05/31/15
    “SI2-SSE: Developing and Deploying Path-Integral Quantum Simulation Tools for a Broad Research Community.” This is a three-year project to develop path integral simulation techniques as a reliable tool for research across many areas of physics.

    NSF DMR 02-39819 Shumway (PI) 03/01/03 – 12/31/09.
    “CAREER: Path Integral Simulations of the Electronic, Optical, and Magnetic Properties of Semiconductor Nanostructures.” This was a five-year single investigator project to develop new computer simulation techniques for quantum mechanical properties of semiconductor nanostructures using Path Integral Monte Carlo methods.

    SRC-NRI SWAN (2006-2009)

    SRC-NRI, Sanjay Banerjee, UT Austin (PI). ''SouthWest Academy of Nanoelectronics (SWAN)''. One of three national centers that comprise the Semiconductor Research Corporation (SRC) Nanoelectronics Research Initiative (NRI). The center is lead by University of Texas at Austin and includes Arizona State, Notre Dame, Maryland, Texas A&M, Texas Tech, Rice, and UT-Dallas.

    DARPA/ONR (2001-2003)

    DARPA/ONR N00014-01-1-1062 Ceperley (PI) 09/20/01 Ð 12/15/03, my role: Co-Investigator Development and Application of Accurate Quantum Simulation Techniques for Materials Design The goal of this study was to develop new quantum Monte Carlo algorithms for materials design with an emphasis on ab-initio simulation of magnetic properties
